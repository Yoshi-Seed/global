What this initiative is

Rare disease research in Japan often requires extra care: smaller populations, higher participant burden, and a need for trust. We built this initiative so global teams can access patient and caregiver perspectives ethically and efficiently.

We can support one-off interviews as well as ongoing programs (journey mapping, unmet needs, treatment decision drivers, support ecosystem). The approach is always tailored to the patient population and the questions you need answered.

Typical outputs

  • Discussion guide / screening logic (EN + JP as needed)
  • Interview execution (remote / in-person where feasible)
  • English debrief + key themes (with nuance)
  • Reporting deck + recommended next questions

Note: specific recruitment routes and feasibility depend on condition, geography, and consent requirements.

How it works

A simple operating model — with transparency at each step.

  1. 01

    Design & alignment

    Define objectives, eligibility, interview approach, and deliverables. We share feasibility considerations early.

  2. 02

    Recruitment planning

    Plan ethical recruitment routes and screening flow. We prioritize participant understanding and informed consent.

  3. 03

    Interviews

    Conduct interviews with sensitivity. Provide interpretation support for live attendance when appropriate.

  4. 04

    Synthesis & reporting

    Translate insights into clear themes and implications. We include what we’re confident about — and what needs follow-up.

Participant-first principles

Research quality improves when people feel safe, respected, and heard.

Clarity

Plain-language explanations, consent, and expectations — before we begin.

Sensitivity

Interview design that respects burden, fatigue, and emotional load.

Privacy

Secure handling of participant information and controlled access to materials.
