Research in Japan requires more than execution. It requires alignment — on expectations, feasibility, timelines, and local realities. Our role is to make that alignment visible from the start.

Our operating model

We follow a clear, step-by-step framework that allows global teams to plan with confidence.

  • Kick-off and expectation alignment
  • Realistic feasibility assessment
  • Recruitment and coordination
  • Japanese–English moderation framework
    (IDI / FGD / online)
  • Transcription and translation (as required)
  • Analysis, reporting, and debrief

Each stage is managed with documented communication and defined deliverables.

Typical timeline

Project timelines vary by target and methodology. Most qualitative studies follow a structured 5–8 week framework, including recruitment, fieldwork, and reporting.

Transparency in scope changes

Research conditions can evolve.

When they do, we address changes directly.

Any adjustments to scope, recruitment feasibility, or timeline are discussed openly before implementation.

No surprises. No hidden assumptions.

Tips for Japan Market Success

What we've learned. These aren't rules—they're patterns we've observed across hundreds of projects. They might change your approach.

01

Speed Isn't Strategy

The fastest path to market in Japan often isn't the right one. Regulatory approval, stakeholder alignment, and cultural fit take time. We help you invest that time wisely.

02

Data Needs Context to Be Meaningful

A 60% preference rate means something different in Japan than in other markets. We help you read the numbers through the lens of Japanese decision-making.

03

The Unspoken Matters More

What people say in surveys and what they actually do are often different. We dig into the gap—that's where real insight lives.

04

Relationships Are Your Infrastructure

In Japan, trust is built slowly and lost quickly. We've spent 40 years building relationships that protect yours.

05

Say No Early

If something won't work in Japan, we tell you before you invest. That's not pessimism—it's partnership.
