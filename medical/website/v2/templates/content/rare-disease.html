Rare Disease Initiative

Patient interviews in rare disease require extra care — ethical recruitment, participant protection, and nuance‑aware reporting. We built this initiative to make that work smoother for global teams.

Past initiatives

We support rare disease studies in Japan with a practical, human approach — respecting burden, protecting privacy, and working with the realities of small populations.

  • Diagnosis confirmation pathways and eligibility checks designed to be respectful, not exhausting.
  • Flexible interview options (online / in‑person where appropriate) to reduce burden.
  • Caregiver inclusion when it meaningfully strengthens insight.

Participant‑first approach

For rare disease, “quality” means more than clean data. It means trust, safety, and a fieldwork setup that does not harm.

  • Clear informed consent and transparent incentives aligned with sponsor requirements.
  • Sensitive moderation that preserves nuance without pushing participants beyond comfort.
  • English deliverables that translate meaning, not just words.
